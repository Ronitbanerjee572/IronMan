Catching the Nuclear Missile (The Avengers, 2012)
Tony’s first true "sacrifice" play. He intercepts a nuclear missile headed for Manhattan, steers it through a wormhole into space, and narrowly falls back to Earth before the portal closes. This event triggered the PTSD that drove his character arc for years.

A pure "spectacle" scene. Seeing the Veronica satellite deploy massive armor pieces to lock onto Tony’s suit so he could go toe-to-toe with a rampaging Hulk in Johannesburg is a peak cinematic moment for tech fans.

After learning Bucky killed his parents, Tony engages in a brutal, claustrophobic 2-on-1 fight against Cap and Bucky. The image of Cap's shield smashing Tony’s arc reactor is haunting.

"I love you 3000." (Avengers: Endgame)
The quiet bedtime moment with his daughter, Morgan. This simple phrase became the emotional core of the movie and served as a reminder of what Tony was fighting to protect during the Time Heist.
